### Introduction

Described by [Fribourg, Jones & Koenig (1977*a*)](#ref2). A virus with isometric, RNA-containing particles about 26 nm in diameter. It has a narrow host range, is readily transmissible by manual inoculation and is widespread in potato crops in the Andean region of South America.

### Main Diseases

In most potato cultivars, primary and secondary symptoms are mild or severe
mottles
([Fig. 1](#fig01)).
Sensitive cultivars may react with initial top necrosis, leaf
deformation and/or stunting
([Fig. 2](#fig02), [Fig. 3](#fig03)).
Plants growing under cool conditions
may develop yellow spotting, blotching or more generalized yellowing on leaves.

### Geographical Distribution

Andean region of South America.

### Host Range and Symptomatology

Readily transmissible by inoculation of sap. Host range mostly limited to the
Solanaceae.

*Diagnostic species: Chenopodium amaranticolor* and *C. quinoa.* Immune. : *Lycopersicon chilense* and *Nicandra physalodes.* Systemic vein- clearing, interveinal mosaic and chlorotic spotting ([Fig. 5](#fig05)). : *Nicotiana bigelovii.* Systemic mosaic consisting of dark green blotches. Tip leaves may develop necrotic areas and holes ([Fig. 4](#fig04)). : *N. clevelandii.* Systemic mosaic. *Propagation species: Nicotiana bigelovii* and *N. clevelandii* are suitable species for maintaining cultures and are good sources of virus for purification. *Assay species*: The virus may be assayed in *N. bigelovii* and *N. clevelandii* by determining the proportion of plants that become systemically infected. No host suitable for local lesion assay is known.

### Strains

Two distinct strains are recognized:

*Strain Lm* of
[Fribourg, Jones & Koenig (1977*a*)](#ref2).
The type
strain.

*Strain H* of
[Salazar & Harrison (1978)](#ref8).

These strains are serologically distinguishable but probably cannot be
reliably distinguished by symptoms
on host plants.

### Transmission by Vectors

Transmission occurs by contact. No vector species is known, but possibly
the virus, like other comoviruses, is transmitted by beetles. *Epitrix*
sp. was not a vector
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2)).

### Transmission through Seed

The virus was not transmitted through seed of *Nicotiana bigelovii*
or of potato cv. Renacimiento
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1978](#ref4)).

### Serology

The virus is very immunogenic; rabbit antiserum with a titre of 1/4096
was readily obtained
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2)).
Gel diffusion
tests in 1% agarose are convenient for detecting the virus in crude sap; one
line of precipitate is produced. The latex flocculation and ELISA techniques
are also useful, for example to detect the virus in potato tubers
([Koenig & Bode, 1978](#ref7)).

### Relationships

Strains Lm and H are serologically related but distinguishable, producing
spurs when compared in gel-diffusion tests using antiserum to either
([Salazar & Harrison, 1978](#ref8)).
The virus is serologically distantly related to
[cowpea mosaic](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=197),
[bean pod mottle](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=108),
and
[quail pea mosaic](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=238) viruses,
and is therefore placed
in the [comovirus group](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=199)
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2)).
It also
shares many properties with members of this group that are not related to it
serologically.

### Stability in Sap

In *Nicotiana bigelovii* sap, the thermal inactivation point (10 min)
is 65-70°C, the dilution end-point is more than 10-6 and
infectivity is retained at 20°C for at least 4 weeks.

### Purification

Steere’s butanol/chloroform method or the bentonite method of
[Dunn & Hitchborn (1965)](#ref1)
are both suitable
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2)).

### Properties of Particles

The particles are all the same size but sediment as three components,
empty protein shells (T) and two kinds of nucleoprotein with different amounts
of RNA (M and B).

Sedimentation coefficients (*s*°20,w) (svedbergs): 53
(T), 93 (M), 112 (B)
([Salazar & Harrison, 1978](#ref8)).

*A*260/*A*280 of unfractionated virus: 1.5
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2)).

Buoyant density in CsCl (g/ml): 1.41(M), 1.46 (B)
([Salazar & Harrison, 1978](#ref8)).

### Particle Structure

Particles are isometric, *c.* 25-27 nm in diameter with somewhat
hexagonal outlines. Electron micrographs show particles, some completely,
some partially and some not penetrated by negative stain
([Fig. 6](#fig06)). Detailed
structure of the particles is not known.

### Particle Composition

*Nucleic acid:* RNA, single-stranded, comprising about 27% and 34%
respectively of the weight of M and B particles, estimated by assuming that
the particles contain 60 molecules of each polypeptide species. The two RNA
species have M. Wt of 1.43 x 106 and 2.0 x 106,
estimated by polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis under denaturing conditions;
M particles contain only the smaller species and B particles only the larger
([Salazar & Harrison, 1978](#ref8)).

*Protein:* Particles contain *c.* 3.8 x 106 daltons of
protein. Two polypeptide species of M. Wt 20,800 and 40,100
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2))
or 22,100 and 41,800
([Salazar & Harrison, 1978](#ref8)).

### Relations with Cells and Tissues

No information.

### Notes

Andean potato mottle virus and the
[tymovirus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=214),
[Andean potato latent virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=124),
are contact-transmitted viruses with similar particle size, shape, and properties
in sap, and both are common in potato crops in the Andean region of South
America. However, they can be readily distinguished by differences in host
range and symptomatology in indicator hosts: Andean potato latent virus infects
species in the Chenopodiaceae and Cucurbitaceae, and produces more severe
symptoms in *N. bigelovii* and *N. clevelandii* but less severe
effects on potato cultivars. Moreover, only Andean potato latent virus is
transmitted by the flea beetle *Epitrix* sp. and the two viruses are
serologically unrelated
([Fribourg, Jones & Koenig, 1977*a*](#ref2),
[1977*b*](#ref3);
[Jones & Fribourg, 1977](#ref5),
[1978](#ref6)).
