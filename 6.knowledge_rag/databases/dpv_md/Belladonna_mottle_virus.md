### Introduction

Described by [Bode & Marcus (1959)](#ref1) and [Paul *et al.* (1968)](#ref7). *Synonym*: Tollkirschenscheckungs-Virus ([Paul *et al.,* 1968](#ref7)) An RNA-containing virus with isometric particles *c.* 27 nm in diameter which sediment as two components in the centrifuge. It is readily transmitted by sap inoculation and by the beetle *Epithrix atropae,* and infects species in the Solanaceae. It is found in Europe.

### Main Diseases

Causes mild mottling and sometimes deformation in *Atropa belladonna*
([Fig. 1](#fig01)).

### Geographical Distribution

Europe. Around Braunschweig (northern Germany) and in the Rila mountains (Bulgaria)
([Paul *et al*., 1968](#ref7)).

### Host Range and Symptomatology

122 species in 28 families of dicotyledons have been tested by inoculation of sap.
Most species
of Solanaceae were infected systemically; the only non-solanaceous host found was
*Chenopodium
quinoa,* in which the virus was restricted to the inoculated leaves
([Paul *et* at., 1968](#ref7)).
Symptom expression in all hosts is variable.

*Diagnostic species: Nicotiana tabacum* (tobacco) cvs. Samsun or Xanthi-nc. Inoculated leaves show ring-like necrotic lesions ([Fig. 4](#fig04)); systemically infected leaves show bright vein-clearing followed by vein-banding ([Fig. 2](#fig02), [Fig. 5](#fig05)) or fine veinal necrosis, and mosaic. : *Datura stramonium.* Many necrotic lesions in the inoculated leaves; mosaic or irregular chlorotic areas or necrotic line patterns in systemically infected leaves. *Propagation species: Nicotiana tabacum, Datura stramonium. Assay species*: No reliable local lesion host known.

### Strains

Variants differ in virulence, in the relative proportions of the two sedimenting components
([Paul, 1969](#ref6)),
and in sensitivity to mercury-containing compounds
([Koenig, 1969](#ref5)).

### Transmission by Vectors

An efficient vector is the beetle *Epithrix atropae*
([Jankulowa *et al.,* 1968](#ref4)).
Aphids seem not to transmit the virus
([Bode & Marcus, 1959](#ref1)).

### Transmission through Seed

None found.

### Transmission by Dodder

Not tested.

### Serology

The virus is strongly immunogenic, antisera with titres of 1/1024 in double
diffusion tests are
readily obtained. In Ouchterlony tests the formation of bands of precipitate is
inhibited by the
addition of mercury-containing preservatives *e.g.* Merthiolate
([Koenig, 1969](#ref5)).

### Relationships

The serological relationship between the strains is very close.
Physical data, RNA base
composition, and serological behaviour show that the virus belongs to the
[Andean potato latent virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=124)
subgroup of the
[turnip yellow mosaic virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=230) group
([Jankulowa *et al.,* 1968](#ref4);
[Koenig, 1969](#ref5)).

### Stability in Sap

The virus is inactivated when heated at 80°C for 10 min; sap of infected plants
retains its
infectivity at room temperature for 5 days to 3 weeks depending on how soon the sap
decomposes. The
dilution end-point is 10-6-10-7
([Paul *et al.,* 1968](#ref7)).

### Purification

The virus is stable, occurs in high concentrations in sap and can easily be purified.
Steere’s
chloroform-butanol method of clarification, followed by several cycles of
differential centrifugation,
gives good virus preparations.

### Properties of Particles

([Paul *et al.,* 1968](#ref7);
[Paul, 1969](#ref6)).
Purified preparations contain two classes of particles,
empty non-infective protein shells without RNA (T) and the infective nucleoprotein (B).
Sedimentation
coefficients (*s*20,w) at infinite dilution (svedbergs): 53 (T), 113 (B).
In most
preparations from tobacco the particle ratio T : B is about 1 : 4.

Molecular weights (daltons): 2.2 x 106 (T), 5.2 x 106 (B).

Diffusion coefficients (D20, w x 10-7
cm2 sec-1):
2.3 (T), 1.6 (B).

Partial specific volumes (calculated): 0.74 ml/g (T), 0.69 ml/g (B).

Electrophoretic behaviour: in gels containing various buffers of pH between 6 and 8
the virus
migrates as one component towards the cathode
([Koenig, 1969](#ref5)).

Absorbance at 260 nm (1 mg/ml, 1 cm light path): about 8 for the
normal mixture of T and B components.

*A*260/*A*280: 1.82,
corrected for light scattering, for the normal mixture of T and B components.

Buoyant density: not determined, but the B component is not homogeneous in CsCl gradients
([Paul, 1969](#ref6)).

### Particle Structure

The particles are isometric, *c.* 27 nm in diameter with angular polygonal outlines
([Fig. 3](#fig03)).
The empty shells are penetrated by negative stains used for electron microscopy,
whereas the infective
particles are not. The virus apparently has a structure similar to that of
[turnip yellow mosaic virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=230),
*i.e.* icosahedral symmetry with 32 major morphological subunits
([Paul *et al.,* 1968](#ref7)).

### Particle Composition

*RNA:* Single-stranded with a M. Wt of 2.0 x 106.
Molar percentage of bases:
G17.5±0.3; A22.8±0.2; C32.8±0.3; U26.9±0.2
([Jankulowa *et al.,* 1968](#ref4)).
The infective particles contain about 37% RNA, the unfractionated virus preparations about
28%
([Paul *et al.,* 1968)](#ref7).

*Protein:* The subunits have a M. Wt of 20,300
(H. L. Paul & U. Buchta, unpublished), and
contain 190-194 amino acid residues. The amino acid composition is (moles %):
ala 8.5; arg 2.5; asx 5.6;
cys 1.1; glx 8.8; gly 6.9; his 0.0; ile 9.1; leu 9.5; lys 4.9; met 0.9; phe 2.6; pro 8.3; ser 12.5; thr
8.4; try 0.6; tyr 2.1; val 7.7
([Jankulowa *et al.,* 1968](#ref4)).

### Relations with Cells and Tissues

The vesicles produced in chloroplasts
([Harrison & Roberts, 1970](#ref3))
resemble those caused by
[turnip yellow mosaic virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=230)
in other species.

### Notes

Some naturally infected *Atropa belladonna* plants also contain
[belladonna mosaic virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=346)
(=[tobacco rattle virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=346))
([Smith, 1943](#ref8)),
[potato leafroll virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=291)
(O. Bode, unpublished) and
[atropa mild mosaic virus](/dpv/showdpv/?dpvno=095)
([Bode, Brandes & Paul, 1969](#ref2);
[Harrison & Roberts, 1970](#ref3)),
which has filamentous
particles about 900 nm long and is transmitted by aphids,
such as *Myzus persicae,* in brief
feeding periods.
